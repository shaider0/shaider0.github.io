About Me

Software Developer | UX Enthusiast

Thanks for visiting my portfolio site! To tell you a little bit about me, I was born in Bangladesh, and moved to the US when I was 4 with my parents and sister. I grew up in a small town called Acton and have lived in Boston for the last 7 years with my wonderful wife. My family is the most important thing in my life, and that includes my 2 nephews and 3 nieces who fill my heart with joy.

After finishing my undergrad in 2010 and through 2018, my professional life was focused on clinical trial project management. I worked at various companies, including Mass General Hospital, Genzyme, and Alexion, running trials, collecting data, and helping bring new drugs to market. This experience taught me how to collaborate, how to lead, how to solve complex problems, and much much more.

In 2018, driven by a passion for technology, I began to teach myself how to code and quickly fell in love. In 2019, I ended up leaving my job and enrolling full-time in General Assembly’s software engineering immersive program in Boston. After 12 weeks, daily coding lessons and three full stack projects, I came away with a solid foundation of software development skills and a few life-long friends.

After General Assembly, I spent several months building a prototype for what would eventually become JoyList, an app I launched with the help of a software development partner (more on that below). Building JoyList taught me a ton about what it takes to launch a product from end to end. It’s been an incredible experience that has pushed me to go beyond coding and learn about product management, UX design, and marketing.

I’m now back focused on coding and currently looking for my first full-time software development role. To brush up on fundamentals, I completed Harvard’s Intro to Computer Science course late in 2020, as well as Colt Steele’s Web Developer Bootcamp in 2021. I’m hopeful that my strong technical foundation combined with my commitment to continuous learning and openness to coaching will help me land my first full-time role as a software developer. If you have an opening that you think I might be a good fit for, please get in touch!
